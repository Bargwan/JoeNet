# Rules of Joe (Imperfect-Information Rummy Variant)

## 1. Setup & The Deal
* **The Deck:** Played with two standard French decks shuffled together (104 cards total). 
* **The Deal:** 11 cards are dealt face-down to each player.
* **The Board:** The remaining cards are placed face-down to form the **Stock Pile**. The top card is flipped face-up to start the **Discard Pile**.
* **Turn Order:** Play proceeds clockwise. The dealer rotates clockwise at the end of each round.

## 2. Round Objectives
The game consists of 7 sequential rounds. To "go down," a player must precisely fulfill the round's objective in their hand.
* **A "3" (Set):** A meld of at least 3 cards of the exact same rank, regardless of suit. (Can contain more than 3 cards).
* **A "4" (Run):** A continuous sequence of at least 4 cards of the exact same suit. 
    * Aces can be played High (`J-Q-K-A`) or Low (`A-2-3-4`).
    * **No Wrap-around:** Runs cannot bridge the King/Two gap (e.g., `K-A-2-3` is invalid). 

**The 7 Round Objectives:**
1. Two 3s `[3, 3]`
2. One 3, One 4 `[3, 4]`
3. Two 4s `[4, 4]`
4. Three 3s `[3, 3, 3]`
5. Two 3s, One 4 `[3, 3, 4]`
6. One 3, Two 4s `[3, 4, 4]`
7. Three 4s `[4, 4, 4]`
*(Note: Objectives are strict. A player cannot play three 3s if the objective is two 3s).*

## 3. Standard Turn Flow
Unless interrupted by a May-I, a standard turn consists of:
1. **Pickup:** Draw the top card from either the Stock Pile or the Discard Pile.
2. **Action (Optional):** Go down (if the objective is met) and/or play "key cards" to extend table melds.
3. **Discard:** Place one card face-up on the Discard Pile.

## 4. The "May-I" Mechanic
A "May-I" is the only way a player can pick up a discard when it is not their turn. Each player is strictly limited to a maximum of **3 May-Is per round**. 

**Resolution Protocol (The Priority Queue):**
1. **The Trigger:** A May-I round is only triggered if the active player declines the top discard and touches the Stock Pile. (If the active player picks up the discard, the May-I opportunity is permanently voided).
2. **Eligibility:** The active player and the player who just discarded the card are strictly ineligible to May-I. Furthermore, players who have already "gone down" forfeit their right to May-I.
3. **The Queue:** The opportunity to May-I passes sequentially downstream (clockwise), starting with the player immediately to the left of the active player.
4. **Resolution:** * If a player accepts the May-I, they receive the targeted discard **plus one penalty card** from the top of the Stock Pile. Their hand size increases by 2. The May-I loop immediately ends.
    * If a player refuses, the option passes to the next downstream eligible player.
5. **Resuming Play:** Whether a player successfully claimed the May-I or all eligible players passed, the active player now completes their draw from the Stock Pile, and their normal turn continues.

*(Note: Players who have already "gone down" forfeit their right to call or block May-Is).*

## 5. Going Down & Going Out
* **Going Down:** Once a player achieves the exact round objective, they may place those melds face-up on the table during their turn. 
* **Extending (Key Cards):** Once down, a player may place additional cards ("key cards") from their hand onto any existing face-up melds or sequences on the table (their own or opponents'). 
* **Going Out:** The round ends immediately when any player discards or plays the last card in their hand. (A player can go "down and out" in a single turn).

## 6. Deck Exhaustion
If the Stock Pile is completely exhausted before a player goes out, the Discard Pile is reshuffled (leaving the top card in place) to form a new Stock Pile, and play continues.

## 7. Scoring
When a player goes out, they score **0 points**. All other players receive penalty points for **every single card remaining in their physical hands** (cards already placed on the table do not count). 

**Crucial Rule:** It does not matter if a player is holding valid melds or sequences that meet the objective. If they have not officially "gone down" and placed those cards face-up on the table before the round ends, those cards count as penalty points.

* **Ranks 2 through 7:** 5 points each
* **Ranks 8 through King:** 10 points each
* **Aces:** 20 points each

The player with the lowest cumulative score at the end of Round 7 wins the game.
